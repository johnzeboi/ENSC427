Simulation of DDoS Attacks on Real-Time Gaming Traffic and QoS

Team 19: John Alanes, Ethan Phan

Abstract

Real-time online games are highly sensitive to network performance, making them easy targets for Distributed Denial-of-Service (DDos) attacks, which greatly disrupt the Quality of Service (QoS) and the player experience. Past research shows the evolution of DDoS techniques, growing the role of botnets and the challenges of detecting and mitigating attacks in network environments. Application-layer attacks in Massively Multiplayer Online (MMO) games have become a large target since they exploit in-game interactions to generate fake traffic surges to flood the game server bandwidths. Studies on gaming QoS show the importance of latency, jitter and packet loss in ensuring playability for online players. Emerging approaches such as Software-Defined Networking (SDN) and Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) offer adaptive, real-time mitigation strategies capable of preserving network performance under a DDoS attack. This research simulates DDoS attacks on online gaming traffic to measure its impact on QoS parameters to evaluate the effectiveness of modern DDoS mitigation techniques. By incorporating the various research on DDoS evolution, gaming traffic behaviour and adaptive defense strategies, this paper seeks to clarify how gaming networks can protect performance when under a DDoS attack.
